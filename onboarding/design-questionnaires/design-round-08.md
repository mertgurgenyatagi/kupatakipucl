# Kupatakip UCL — Design Questionnaire, Round 8 (final)

Last round, a bit more concrete, filled out via the design-intake tool.

---

**1. The white half.** For the white half of that navy/white split — pure white, a slightly cool off-white, or a slightly warm off-white?

Your answer: Very very very very very very very slightly beige.

---

**2. Deep stats, when.** For the deep stats stuff (lineups, xG, tournament breakdowns) — fine launching without it and adding later, or is it something you want tackled early?

Your answer: Fine without it at first, add later

---

**3. Numbers lining up.** Numbers everywhere — points, rankings, stats — should they line up in neat aligned columns everywhere, or just where it obviously matters (like the leaderboard)?

Your answer: Only where it obviously matters

---

**4. One more reference.** You've named a few real ones by now (linear.app, The Telegraph, satoshiwatanabe.org) — anything else that's been sitting in your head you haven't said yet?

Your answer: huts.com and I'll explain why.

I don't want something extremely simple. Listen to the following words carefully: We want a modern website. The only difference from a modern website is it's compact and easy to intuitively understand. But absolutely sophisticated. Text sliding and fading in, small animations. Definitely not a static HTML.

---

**5. Mobile, specifically.** On mobile specifically — is anything actually different from everything we've talked about, or just the same site, smaller?

Your answer: It's a completely different beast. If we're emphasizing intuition, say, 60 percent on desktop, on mobile it should be 120 percent. Design must never obscure information.

---

**6. Three words.** Sum up the whole direction in exactly three words, right now, no overthinking it.

Your answer: Modern, cool, compact.

---

**7. How finished at launch.** At launch: a polished v1 that already looks fully 'done,' or something that visibly still has room to grow?

Your answer: Polished, looks done

---

**8. Anything left unsaid.** Anything left unsaid before we actually start building this?

Your answer: On desktop, I have a very controversial idea. Very controversial. I'm even scared of saying it. Are you ready? Here it goes:

No vertical scrolling.

I know, I know, I know. Practically every modern website has vertical scrolling. But wouldn't it be great if we could just contain everything into one screen? Two things are obvious: 1) Clearly partial vertical scrolling will need to exist at some level, but I would love a no-global-scrolling website. 2) Maybe little subpages that go horizontally? Not sure not sure not sure.
